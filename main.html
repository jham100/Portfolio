Stanford University

 September 2019 - Present

I am currently a Software Developer at Stanford University, within the Land, Buildings & Real Estate Department. I work with a team of Software Developers, Project Managers, QA Testers and GIS specialists to build and support applications that facilitate the management and use of Stanford University's physical assets. Applications that I have personally worked on include: the Stanford Searchable Campus Map, the Facilities Information Management System, the Dish Area website, the Land, Buildings & Real Estate website and dozens of apps that are inaccessible to the public.

Rosemary Gratch Memorial

This web application is a living memorial to my late grandmother, Rosemary Gratch. Users of the application can view curated images and videos of Rosemary and her loved ones. Users can leave comments, share direct links to their favorite items, and add captions depending on their access level. I used HTML, CSS, JS / jQuery, Google Firebase (Hosting, Firestore, Authentication), and Node.js (via Google Cloud Functions) in the development of this project.

My Story


I am originally from New York, where I graduated from SUNY Purchase in 2016 with a BA in Economics.

I relocated to Oakland, CA in early 2017 to fill an administrative position at Monterey Tilth: a large-scale cannabis cultivation company based in Salinas, CA.

I became fully immersed in software development when my role at Monterey Tilth shifted to capturing and managing their data, eventually developing an internal application for mission-critical business operations and analytics.

I attended the UC Berkeley Extention Full-Stack Web Development program from February to August 2018 to widen my understanding of web development.

In September of 2019, I began a contract role as a Software Developer at Stanford University | Land, Buildings & Real Estate. In this role, I work with a team of Software Developers, Project Managers, QA Testers and GIS specialists to build and support applications that facilitate the management and use of Stanford University's physical assets.

In March of 2020, I became a full-time employee of Stanford University.
